In-Vehicle Media Experience Research

KAIST (KIXLAB) & Hyundai Motor Group

In the summer of 2021, I participated as a researcher for a "In Car Media Experience" project with KAIST Interaction Lab and Hyundai Motor Group.

During these 3 months, I research various in-car media experiences people would like to have in cars. The main research processes are as below!

  • Hyundai Motor Group Workshop
  • User Workshop on In-Vehicle Media Experiences
  • In-Vehicle Media Experience Prototype Development




    • Hyundai Motor Group Workshop

    At the start of the research, we had a workshop with Hyundai Motor Group and various media professionals like game graphic designers, sound engineers, and producers. The workshop took place at the Hyundai Motor Group headquarters.

    In this workshop we discussed the current development plans of Hyundai with in-car media experiences and the feasibility of implementing those plans into cars. It was an eye-opening experience to discuss with various media professionals about the need for certain designs and the expected challenges with implementing these designs.

    I realized that in-car media experiences coupled with car interior design and consideration of the moving space would prove to be helpful in creating in-vehicle media experiences.

    The goal of this co-op was to conduct user research on effective in-car media experiences and design prototypes for future user testing.


    User Workshop

    The first step of this user workshop was to conduct a focus group workshop to understand the user-needs regarding in-car media experiences. After conducting some paper research we settled on a workshop plan.

    Workshop Plan

    The workshop would consist of 3-4 people and take place online through a zoom call. We would be conducting the research on a miroboard.

    User Recruitment

    We recruited the users through list-serv emails with a google form. In the recruitment process, we divided the users into various groups : driver vs passenger / amount of time spent in car / age groups...etc. In coming up with the final research groups, we did our best to mix a variety of user groups together. Additionally, in the recruitment survey, we added survey questions on specific media experiences. Later in our analysis we used these survey questions to analyze our user results.
